About

Designing elegant, empowering experiences in New York City.

Hey, I'm Kathleen. A continual learner with diverse interests, I draw connections across disciplines and approach each situation through multiple lenses. My desire to design elegant, empowering experiences stems from witnessing both the transformative potential of innovation and the anxiety it can cause. As a researcher conducting interviews with entrepreneurs in the Middle East and North Africa, I had many conversations that attested to the ability of technology to create positive change. While helping clients navigate a company's online applications, I became acutely aware of the fear and frustration caused by digital experiences, even those intended to be a tool to improve people's lives. I believe that innovation should benefit everyone it impacts, and want everyone who uses products I create to feel the same.

Process

Problem solving is my main goal as a UX designer, and communication + collaboration underpin my process.

1. Evaluate

Through research, I refine my initial problem statement until I discover the core of the issue.

2. Build

I generate solutions, build prototypes, and continue testing + iterating until designs are validated.

Work

No two projects are the same.

I love the challenge of choosing the best tools to evaluate a given situation, and then translating research insights into design solutions.

Mayday

Mayday is a Political Action Committee (PAC) dedicated to fighting corruption and ending the influence of money in politics. With two team members, I researched and designed an app that increases Mayday's potential impact while enabling users to take concrete action in order to make their voices heard.

Nakul Karkare, MD

Nakul Karkare, MD is a New York-based orthopedic surgeon. With a team of two others designers, I redesigned Dr. Karkare’s website in order to better align the site’s structure and content with the needs of his patients.

Lunch Under

For busy New Yorkers, finding lunch that is affordable, tasty, and close by can be a daily hassle. Lunch Under is an app designed to help users find quality lunch options within their budget and near their location, and to take some of the risk out of trying new lunch spots.
